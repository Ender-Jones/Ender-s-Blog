### June Close

#### Theme

June was supposed to be journal-writing month. Instead a five-day conference deadline landed on the 10th, and the month turned into three deliveries back to back — with the journal advancing only in the gaps between them.

#### What Changed

**Experiments / Research**

- A full conference paper went from "no experiments at all" to a GO on three contributions in roughly 24 hours: rPPG reliability under social stress, retraining-free fusion, and HRV recoverability.
- Verification scripts were written alongside the experiments, not after: HR derivation, alignment/tags, and a fusion no-leakage check.
- Findings that held up: classical methods beat the deep family here; fusion saturates after two or three good methods; per-subject calibration rescues single methods but not fusion; HRV is not recoverable — which is what points the work toward EDA.
- None of this touched the journal's assets or protocol. The conference paper was carved out of a screening step whose result was already null for the journal.

**Paper / Publication**

- The publication program got locked into a chain instead of three separate efforts: the earlier short paper as preliminary, the conference paper as companion, the journal as capstone.
- The advisor confirmed the baseline author is not a co-author.
- The conference paper was cancelled after submission — the attendance fee was judged too high. Its reliability audit was folded into the journal as motivation rather than becoming a standalone companion, and it added no new contribution.
- Journal status by month end: front matter finalized, §III.A done, §III.B half written.

**Public Site / Blog**

- Effectively frozen all month. The V2 site took its last commit on the 8th; V3 did not start until July.

#### Carry Into July

- Journal is the only line: finish §III.B–E, then IV–VII.
- The next-journal direction (vision-language on a larger dataset) stays parked until the current draft is out.
